Personal facts about me

I'm Sara. A Colombian girl that loves sicence, music, dance and coding. Here are some curious experiences I have had in my life:

I didn't wanted to go to the school anymore

When I was 14 years old, I told my parents that I didn't wanted to go to the school anymore. I felt like I was wasting my time and that there was so many things I wanted to learn.
So, I ended my high-school online while taking classes of piano, hip-hop, jazz dance, korean and german ( languages that I will totally forget later hah).

How I learned not to fear people
I guess this must have been around the time of when I was 12 years old. I just started learning music and piano here in Los Andes, but that was almost a miracle because my audition didn’t go well. The audition was directed by the prime master Maria Barbara (an old woman with about 80 years) and I had never played a real acoustic piano before.

So I played a song I learnt on Youtube in the worst possible way a song can be played in a Steinway piano. This scary old woman said I sat like I was in a bus. Then, she said I was too old to start learning piano, because kids usually start learning at 6. But, finally she said “ok, you can start learning. But always remember you can switch to an easier instrument”. Off course, after this audition every time I saw Maria Barbara I got paralyzed remembering all the things she said to me that day.

But one day, it was the final exam day and my father came to cheer me up. When it was my turn, my father waited outside, I entered the room and SHE WAS THERE. When the exam ended, I went out the room red like a tomato and shaking. My father asked me how did it go and I told him I got paralyzed because Maria Barbara was there. He said that it was ok and hugged me. We stayed there for half an hour talking and Maria Barbara went out for a snack. When my father saw her, he reached her and said “Hey I want to talk with you”. I was like “nooooo, what are you doing”. But wait a minute. It gets better. He TOLD HER THAT I WAS AFRAID OF HER. She just laughed and said "BOOH!". Yeah sure, very funny -_- .

Since that day, I became very close to Maria Barbara and started learning a lot from her. Four years later, I started wining piano contests and she told me that I PLAYED IN A REALLY PROFFESIONAL WAY. I realized how my fear was preventing me from learning. She was right in everything she said in my audition, but it was only when I left my fear, that I started to improve.

Thank you Maria Barbara, RIP.
